📋 How Montgomery County Tax Sale Works — Investor Quick Reference ▼

🏛 What You Buy

You purchase a tax lien certificate, not the property itself. The county's lien transfers to you. The owner can redeem (pay you back) with interest, or you eventually foreclose and take title.

📅 Sale Date & Bidding

  • Held annually — 2nd Monday of June
  • Sealed bid process
  • Minimum bid = amount advertised
  • Must purchase minimum 10 liens per bidder
  • Payment via electronic funds transfer

💵 Interest Rates

  • 6% per annum — owner-occupied homes
  • 20% per annum — all other properties
  • Interest accrues daily from sale date
  • Paid by owner upon redemption

⏳ Redemption Timeline

  • Owner-occupied: 9 months before foreclosure
  • Non-owner-occupied: 6 months before foreclosure
  • Certificate valid for 2 years — must file before expiry
  • ~6 months after sale: certificate issued

⚠ High-Bid Premium

If your bid exceeds 40% of the FCV, a 20% premium is charged on the excess amount. Properties flagged ⚠️ in this tool have amount-due > 40% of FCV — premium risk on any winning bid.

🎯 Flip Strategy

  • Low LTV = most equity cushion for flip
  • After foreclosure, take title & resell
  • Grade A+/A = best equity-to-cost ratio
  • Commercial liens often redeemed at 20%
  • Check title for senior liens before bidding
